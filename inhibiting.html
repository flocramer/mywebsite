1. Money as an inhibiting factor

Its sad to hear but oftentimes museums can not rely on their ticket sales as enough income for the museum to sustain. This can also lead to little to no changes in exhibitions or the museum's display. To change there is the necessity for staff to work on this, and for this staff to be paid. It could be that volunteers are a part of the working staff. Volunteers can be incredibly useful and beneficial for a museum however they are not professionals. They are not involved or possibly not educated on the museum's artefacts and their possible sensitivity.

2. Staff

Another reason why museums might not change their exhibitions ties together with money as an inhibiting factor. For too little money can also lead to less staff members. It is the case that staff members have certain tasks that need to happen such as work with visitors or give tours. There could be that there is not enough time to also be working on changing exhibitions, texts or artefacts. The displays seemingly do not change until mor staff can help with the workload
